# Vision & Mission

## Vision

A world where **no one is excluded from digital life or daily living because of a disability and a lack of money.** Wherever an expensive proprietary tool exists, a free, private, excellent open-source equivalent should exist too.

## Mission

To build and maintain, as a commons, a **suite of 100% free and open-source assistive applications** covering the full range of disabilities — motor, sensory, cognitive, language, age-related — usable everywhere, including offline and on modest hardware.

## The problem, in numbers

- About **1.3 billion people** live with a significant disability (WHO) — roughly 16% of humanity.
- WHO estimates **2.5 billion people** need at least one assistive technology, and **900 million will lack access** by 2050 at the current pace.
- In low-income countries, only about **3%** of people who need assistive products get a suitable one.
- Costs are prohibitive: a serious AAC app costs €200–400, eye-gaze solutions run into thousands, some screen-magnification software costs hundreds.

## What we believe

1. **Accessibility is a right, not a premium feature.** It is enshrined in the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD).
2. **Code that helps the most vulnerable should be a commons** — auditable, repairable, translatable by anyone, forever.
3. **Privacy is non-negotiable** when designing for children, patients, and dependent people.
4. **"Nothing about us, without us."** Disabled people are co-designers, not passive recipients.
5. **Offline and frugal**, because the most acute needs are often where money and connectivity are scarce.

## What we will never do

- No **SaaS**, no features locked behind a subscription.
- No **ads**, no **data sales**, no **tracking**.
- No **dependency** on a proprietary service for a core function to work.
- No **broken promises** to fragile audiences: what we ship is tested with them.

## Definition of success (5-year horizon)

- At least **8 mature applications**, translated into **10+ languages**, covering all major disability domains.
- `a11y-core` reused by **third-party projects** (evidence of ecosystem-wide leverage).
- **Real-world deployments**: schools, nonprofits, hospitals, homes — including low-connectivity regions.
- **Sustainable governance and funding** guaranteeing free access in perpetuity.
